Readiness Check

One challenge we have seen with offshoring projects is an organisation kicking off things without a solid understanding of what is involved, nor having the tools and processes in place to be successful.

A readiness check should give you perspective and the reality of your organisations' current state. Further, it should provide you and your team with a clearly defined plan of action that is designed to ensure the success of the offshoring initiative.

We recommend a workshop that should be used as a forum to discuss and evaluate;

  • Business goals, objectives, timelines and success criteria for future measurement(s)
  • Current and future opportunities to offshore within the business
  • Current processes that are relevant to your offshoring opportunities and core business function(s)
  • Items that may pose challenges or risks to the offshore initiatives

You should come away from your readiness check with clarity around the projects vision and approach

Process Changes

Once we understand your risks we will recommend and facilitate change to achieve the best results from your offshoring initiatives. The scope of these projects could range from minor process changes or organisational structure recommendations, or new systems for increased collaboration and visibility across the business.

The recommendations outlined in the readiness check report will likely contain changes to your processes and/or systems to support an offshored, co-located operation.

The objectives of this phase are to;

  • Define and implement any process changes the business agrees are desirable and/or critical to the success of the offshoring initiative
  • Communicate and manage progress throughout the process including weekly reviews and project updates
  • Updated Risk Management matrix are risk items are managed and their risk criteria shifts

This phase can be completed in parallel to supplier setup and recruitment so long as risks are understood and communicated clearly to all stakeholders.

Partner Selection

Different operational functions have different requirements, whether that be timezone coverage, required hours or job responsibilities. These attributes may lead your project to a different supplier and we will be able to provide you with recommendations and introductions to ensure that you are engaging with the best supplier for your requirements.

Change Fox has a number of suppliers that it can recommend depending on your requirements. We do however strongly recommend some time on the ground, meeting prospective suppliers to understand how they operate and the working environment your team will be operating in.

During this phase we can facilitate and optionally participate throughout the following;

  • Introductions via email, skype or teleconference
  • Onsite meetings with prospective suppliers
  • Facilitate indicative staffing proposals and costings
  • Understand your contractual obligations with suppliers
Team Building

It is critical to not only hire for the right capabilities but also cultural fit to your organisation. We recognise this and will take the time to understand your business culture and provide our expertise through the team building process. We introduce amazing people to our clients and the Change Fox Community.

Once you have set up your supplier relationship, it’s time to start building your team. We strongly recommend that interviews be conducted in person however you can alternatively have Change Fox conduct a cultural fit interview and provide feedback into the decision-making process to be done remotely.

The objective of this phase is to;

  • Development or revision and updates of job description(s) and job advertisement(s)
  • Candidate screening and shortlisting
  • Development of interview questions and success criteria
  • Initial interviews and shortlisting
  • Facilitate interviews either onsite or remotely via Skype with you and your team
  • Provide recommendations on staffing choices and alignment with organisational requirements and culture
