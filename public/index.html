What? Who? Where?

What is Offshoring?

Offshoring is the strategic relocation of operational functions to other locations, usually countries with economical advantages such as; lower wages or business incentives.

Importantly offshoring differs from outsourcing in that you are adding new staff to your team! With the help and support of your offshoring partner, you maintain control over workload, training and performance outcomes as you would a local team member.

Who Should Offshore?

The move towards offshoring was largely driven by multi-nationals seeking competitive advantage in industries where every cent matters, but that does not mean that smaller organisations should not explore offshoring as well!

Offshoring need not be an exercise of building a team of hundreds, or even tens of new team members. It is normal for SME's to start with the "low hanging fruit" such as;

  • Tele-marketing & tele-sales
  • Virtual Assistants
  • Back office functions
  • Administrative
  • Customer service, voice or non-voice
  • Helpdesk support, voice or non-voice

After you and your organisation are confident with your offshored team, we usually see a transition to suppliment other, more technical roles, within the business including;

  • Technical support
  • Software development
  • NOC and SOC agents

Where Should You Offshore?

There are a number of locations globally that have become offshoring hubs such as; Malaysia, China & India but in recent years the largest amounts of growth and industry maturity has been seen in the Philippines and Vietnam.

  • Philippines
  • Vietnam
  • Others

The Philippines has a history of western "integration" that is a strength in terms of cultural appreciation and English proficiency. Further, the countries recent development has been largely fuelled by the BPO industry with a reporting 60% of registered businesses working in, or servicing the industry.

Some of the worlds largest call centers are either headquartered or serviced from the Philippines with over one million jobs in the industry, primarily in call centers, but reports also suggest that the industry is someone stagnant with growth numbers levelling out in recent years.

Driven by large amounts of foreign investment and a great combination of culture, government incentives and wage benefits, Vietnam is seen by many as the next global BPO powerhouse.

Recruited talent have often studied at international schools or abroad, while returning Vietnamese bring a strong understanding of western culture, customer expectations and also strong English proficiency.

Readiness Check

One challenge we have seen with offshoring projects is an organisation kicking off things without a solid understanding of what is involved, nor having the tools and processes in place to be successful in the long-term. This is a huge contributing factor to any offshoring projects that fail to meet expectations.

A readiness check should give you perspective and highlight the reality of your organisations' current state. Further, it should provide you and your team with a clearly defined plan of action that is designed to ensure the success of the offshoring initiative.

We recommend a workshop that should be used as a forum to discuss and evaluate;

  • Business goals, objectives, timelines and success criteria for future measurement(s)
  • Current and future opportunities to offshore within the business
  • Current processes that are relevant to your offshoring opportunities and core business function(s)
  • Items that may pose challenges or risks to the offshore initiatives

You should come away from your readiness check with clarity around the projects vision and approach

Process Changes & Change Management

Once you understand your offshoring objectives and the new tools & processes required to deliver, you will need to facilitate change effectively to achieve the best results from your offshoring project. This must be done while also keeping your local stakeholders excited and keen to be a part of the process.

The objectives of this phase are to;

  • Define and implement any process changes the business agrees are desirable and/or critical to the success of the offshoring project
  • Communicate and manage progress throughout the process including weekly reviews and project updates
  • Maintain project documentation and plans to ensure visibility and transparency

This phase can be completed in parallel to partner selection and team bulding so long as timelines and dependencies are understood and communicated clearly to stakeholders.

Partner Selection

Different operational functions have different requirements, whether that be timezone coverage, required hours or job responsibilities. These attributes may lead your project to a different partner (or multiple suppliers at times) and we will be able to provide you with recommendations and introductions to ensure that you are engaging with the best supplier for your requirements.

A thorough partner selection process is recommended as finding an organisation that you are ethically comfortable and operationally sure of is critical to the success of your offshoring project.

Optionally, you can also engage with an offshoring consultancy firm like Change Fox as they have vetted partners that they will recommend based on your requirements.

During this phase you should consider the follow activities;

  • Partner list sourcing either by onlin research, referrals or an offshoring consulting firm
  • Introductions via email and skype or teleconference
  • Onsite meetings with prospective partners if possible
  • Facilitate indicative staffing proposals and costings
  • Understand your contractual obligations with prospective partners

Team Building

It is critical to not only hire for the right capabilities but also cultural fit to your organisation. You should be looking to ensure integration with your local team and a thorough, comprehensive onboarding process too. The energy and time put into your team building processes will have a direct influence on the results of your project!

Consider creating attractive roles that have opportunities for growth and development, and ensure that your job descriptions and adverts portray your organisational culture and the type of person you are hoping to hire.

We strongly recommend that interviews be conducted in person, however, you can alternatively have your partners conduct a cultural fit interviews and provide feedback into the decision-making process. You would then conduct online interviews for final staff selection.

The objective of this phase is to;

  • Develop, revise and update attractive job description(s) and job advertisement(s)
  • Candidate screening and shortlisting
  • Development of interview questions and candidate success criteria
  • Initial interviews and shortlisting
  • Facilitation of either onsite or remote interviews with you and your team
